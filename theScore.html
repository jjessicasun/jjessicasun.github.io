Diving deeper into platform reliability on ESPN/theScore Bet at theScore

Uncovering pain points and identifying key areas for improvement

RESPONSIBILITIES

Survey design, interview design, user interviews, data analysis, qualitative coding, customer journey mapping

TEAM

1 UX Research Manager, 1 UX Research Mentor, 1 Technical Product Manager

TIMELINE

May - August 2024

Project Overview

Context

I joined as a UX Research Intern on the Wagering team, focusing on enhancing the sports betting experience. theScore is a digital sports media platform, offering real-time news and scores. theScore/ESPN Bet is a sportsbook and casino app which integrates directly with theScore app, allowing users to place wagers right from their mobile device.

Role

My primary focus over the term was on identifying and consolidating user pain points and concerns regarding the reliability of our sportsbook. I worked heavily with Technical Product Managers, Engineering Managers, and Product leadership on the Wagering team to make key decisions about the research direction and results.

Outcome

User interviews and extensive analysis of quarterly survey data revealed that the most common pain points users had were: building parlays with more than 4 legs, placing a bet right before a game/event, and logging in. From the company, users value accountability and security, with the main sentiment amongst users being: "A little goes a long way."

Research and recommendations were presented to product leadership and engineering to provide initial insight. Following the conclusion of my term, the project was passed onto full-time UX Researchers to continue.
